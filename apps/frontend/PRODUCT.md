# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Owners of physical, local businesses — barbershops, cafés, gyms, dental clinics, auto shops, restaurants. They are time-poor, not online-native, and have no marketing team. They know their craft cold but have never written a Reddit post, run an ad, or thought about "where their customers talk online." Their context: running a counter, a chair, or a floor all day. They have five minutes, not five hours.

## Product Purpose

Buzz turns a 5-minute conversation into a fully autonomous marketing team. A business describes itself once; Buzz researches their market, finds where their customers actually talk online, writes content in their voice, posts it, and self-improves based on real engagement. Zero human input after intake. Success looks like a business owner who never logs in again still getting found by new customers.

Three agents do the work:
- **Jack** — the intake conversation. Five questions over WhatsApp or a web form, output as a structured business brief.
- **Scout** — long-running research + content. Reads Reddit, finds the audience, drafts and scores a post. Runs 30–45 minutes unattended.
- **Pulse** — posting + self-improvement. Posts to Reddit, monitors engagement every 10 minutes, rewrites when upvotes are low, logs its reasoning.

## Brand Personality

Autonomous, alive, quietly confident. The voice of a system that does the work while you sleep — never breathless, never salesy. It speaks plainly to people who distrust marketing jargon. Three words: **autonomous, alive, unattended.**

## Reference

**background-agents.com** — light editorial "white-paper" feel: serif display, crimson accent,
dotted-grid hero, the SDLC pinch diagram, hairline cards. This is the named target to match.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient blob hero, three pastel feature cards, "Supercharge your X" headline.
- AI-purple-on-white startup template.
- Marketing-agency bravado: stock photos of people pointing at whiteboards.
- Dark "developer tool" control-room theme — wrong register; this is a calm, editorial research note.
- Cream/sand/beige body bg — keep the light field neutral gray (chroma ≤0.004), not warm parchment.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the machine running.** The product's value is that it works unattended — the page should feel alive (a pulse, live status, agents "working"), not static.
2. **Narrate the problem before the product.** Like a self-driving system: establish why localhost / the business owner is the ceiling, then reveal the agents.
3. **One sentence does the heavy lifting.** Lead with the single-sentence idea; let everything expand from it.
4. **Three named agents, three distinct jobs.** Jack / Scout / Pulse are characters with roles, not bullet points.
5. **Plain words for people who hate marketing.** No jargon the owner wouldn't say themselves.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 against the dark surface. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for the pulse and reveal animations. Keyboard-reachable nav and CTAs.
